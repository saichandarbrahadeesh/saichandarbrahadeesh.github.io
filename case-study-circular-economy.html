A business that didn't exist, funded by a reframed pitch

Global Industrial Manufacturer · Circular Economy Offer
The technical cycle — three loops back into the value chain, before the material ever hits landfill
Problem

Ambitions for sustainability were high — real impact against SDG targets and carbon neutrality. In practice, the harder problem was commercial: avoiding cannibalization of existing revenue streams, protecting profitability, and building a parallel system that could run as seamlessly as the open-loop manufacturing process it sat beside.

  • No supply process. No factory line. No pricing model. No budget.
  • Post-pandemic: every capital request scrutinized. Sustainability-led pitches had been refused twice — they read as a cost line, not a return.
Impact
  • The new business model created an incremental revenue stream, with net profitability up to 30% higher than the traditional offer.
  • World Economic Forum named the offer a Lighthouse Project.
Approach

Single point of orchestration — operating like a founder inside a large company, with no team handed to me. Every function that needed to touch this offer (Legal, Supply Chain, Industrialisation, Branding, Communications, Industrial Design, Sustainability, country sales) I had to convince, align, and coordinate individually.

Reframed the pitch. Prior refusals had led with "sustainability" as the value. I pivoted the narrative: a refurbished-product line is a financial hedge against supply-chain shocks. When raw materials inflate, a second inventory stream lets you keep shipping to customers at healthy margin. Sustainability was also made into sustaining business, sustaining profits, and sustaining the planet.

  • Won corporate budget in a 10-minute pitch to senior leadership after two refusals.
  • Scouted usable supply from recalls and cancelled orders. Ran a conjoint-analysis study with 100+ customers to set pricing and warranty terms.
  • Earned LCA certification (Bureau Veritas) myself to own the environmental case directly in customer conversations.
Principle

A big enough mission doesn't stop at a closed door — it checks for the window. Reframe the pitch, track inventory by hand if that's what it takes, do whatever the venture actually needs. Commercial success comes first; the system to support it gets built after.
