Hardware × Software Integration

An industry-first switchboard, with no playbook to follow

Global Industrial Manufacturer · Connected Hardware + Cloud Software
3-layer architecture — data flowing product → gateway → software
Problem
  • Industry-first integration: circuit breakers, wireless sensors, and cloud dashboard talking natively over LoRaWAN. No playbook.
  • Inherited mid-flight: MVP scope set, launch date fixed, timeline slipping, 50+ features queued.
Impact
  • Shipped the industry's first natively-connected switchboard on the original date.
  • Cut 75% of the feature backlog to ensure timely delivery to market.
Approach

As Product Lead, I owned the complete system value across hardware and software — not just the documentation, the actual design decisions connecting circuit breaker, edge gateway, and energy management software into one coherent flow.

  • Cut 75% of the backlog against three criteria: no commercial path, out of MVP scope, or purely cosmetic — to ensure timely delivery to market.
  • Redirected 6 mobile developers off the configuration tool and onto other projects. There was no business case for polishing a tool further while the real value proposition — the connected system itself — was still under pilot test.
Principle

A just-enough product on a quick timeline beats a great product built over years. Speed to market is a feature nobody puts on the spec sheet, but customers feel it first.
